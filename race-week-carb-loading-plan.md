**Race:** Napa Valley Marathon - Sunday, March 1, 2026
**Goal:** Sub-2:50 (6:29/mile pace)

**Carb-Loading Protocol:**
- **Friday (2 days out):** 10-12g/kg bodyweight

- **Sunday (race day):** 2-4g/kg pre-race meal

**For 150 lb (68 kg) runner:**
- Friday: 680-820g carbs

**For 165 lb (75 kg) runner:**
- Friday: 750-900g carbs

## Expected Physical Changes

### Normal During Carb-Loading
- ✓ Weight gain of 2-4 lbs (water stored with glycogen - this is GOOD)
- ✓ Feeling slightly bloated/full
- ✓ More frequent urination
- ✓ Slightly elevated energy
- ✓ Muscles feeling "full"

### Red Flags (Adjust If These Occur)
- ✗ Severe GI distress → reduce fiber/fat further
- ✗ Nausea → spread intake more evenly, smaller portions
- ✗ Extreme bloating → reduce sodium slightly
- ✗ Diarrhea → reduce fiber, stick to very simple carbs

## Scientific Basis

This plan is based on research showing:
- Glycogen supercompensation increases muscle glycogen stores by 30-50%
- Elevated glycogen levels persist for 3+ days with adequate carb intake
- Pre-race carb intake independently influences marathon performance
- During-race carb intake of 60-90g/hour optimizes performance for sub-3:00 marathoners

**Key Studies:**
- Pre-race carbohydrate intake independently influences performance (Nutrition 2011)
- Glycogen supercompensation persists for 3+ days in trained athletes (J Appl Physiol 1997)
- Current recommendations: 10-12g/kg for 36-48 hours pre-race (Sports Med Open 2024)
