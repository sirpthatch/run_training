# Napa Valley Marathon Training Plan - TRAVEL ADJUSTED
**Target Race:** Napa Valley Marathon - March 1, 2026
**Goal:** Sub-2:58 (primary), Sub-2:50 (stretch)
**Target Marathon Pace:** 6:30-6:50/mile
**Start Date:** November 18, 2025 (Week 1)
**Training Weeks:** 15 weeks + race week
**Travel Period:** Dec 22-Jan 5 (Weeks 6-7 modified for maintenance)

## Constraints & Modifications
- **Saturday max:** 6 miles (family commitments)
- **Sunday max:** 13 miles (limited time)
- **Tuesday flexibility:** Up to 3 hours for long runs
- **Current base:** 40-50 mpw
- **Strategy:** Tuesday long runs (18-22 miles) + Sunday quality runs for race-time adaptation

## Training Philosophy

**Key Change from Original Plan:**
- **Tuesday = Primary long run** (builds to 20-22 miles for marathon-specific endurance)
- **Sunday = Quality medium-long run** (13 miles with marathon pace work + race-time adaptation)
- **Wednesday = Quality workout** (tempo, intervals, marathon pace - fitness driver)

This structure provides:
1. Proper long run preparation for the full marathon distance
2. Race-time adaptation (Sunday morning runs simulate race start)
3. Quality midweek workouts to build speed and threshold
4. Higher peak mileage (68-70 mpw) for better aerobic development

## Workout Key

**Easy Pace:** 7:30-8:00/mile (conversational pace, 70-75% max HR)
**Marathon Pace (MP):** 6:30-6:50/mile (race goal pace)
- Sub-2:50 goal: 6:30-6:40/mile
- Sub-2:58 goal: 6:45-6:50/mile
**Tempo Pace:** 6:25-6:45/mile (comfortably hard, ~85% max HR)
**Interval Pace:**
- 400m: 5:40-5:45/mile (~85-86 seconds)
- 800m: 6:00/mile (3:00 per 800m)
- 1600m: 6:10/mile

**Strides:** 80-100m accelerations to 90% effort, full recovery between

## Critical Training Notes

### Recovery & Adaptation
**Tuesday â†’ Wednesday is a key sequence:**
- Tuesday long run depletes glycogen and stresses aerobic system
- Wednesday workout requires proper recovery
- **Essential recovery practices:**
  - Fuel within 30 minutes of Tuesday long run (30-40g protein, 60-100g carbs)
  - Hydrate aggressively Tuesday afternoon/evening
  - 8+ hours sleep Tuesday night
  - Assess readiness Wednesday - if legs are dead, reduce workout intensity

**Thursday is critical recovery day:**
- Keep truly easy (7:45-8:00/mile, no faster)
- This allows weekend runs to be quality

### Long Run Fueling Practice
**Tuesday long runs 90+ minutes:**
- Practice exact race-day fueling
- Start fueling at 45-60 minutes
- 30-60g carbs per hour (1 gel every 30-40 min)
- Hydrate: 4-8 oz every 15-20 minutes

**Test different products weeks 1-6, commit to race-day nutrition weeks 7-15**

### Adjust for Life
**If you miss a workout:**
- Priority 1: Wednesday quality workouts
- Priority 2: Tuesday long runs
- Priority 3: Sunday quality runs
- Don't try to "make up" missed workouts

**If feeling overtrained:**
- Take extra rest day (replace Monday easy run with rest)
- Reduce Wednesday workout intensity
- Never skip recovery weeks

### Wednesday Workout Modifications
**After 20-22 mile Tuesday runs (Weeks 7, 9-11, 13):**
- Slightly reduce volume or intensity if needed
- Focus on quality over quantity
- Listen to your body - heavy legs mean reduce the workout
